# AFTER ACTION REPORT (AAR)
## Operation Pacific Relief 2026
### Humanitarian Assistance / Disaster Relief — Typhoon Response

**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

**Date of Report:** 15 March 2026
**Prepared by:** J3 Operations, USINDOPACOM
**Exercise/Operation Dates:** 28 February – 12 March 2026
**Location:** Republic of the Philippines, Eastern Visayas Region

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

On 27 February 2026, Super Typhoon AMARA made landfall in the Eastern Visayas region of the Philippines with sustained winds of 155 mph, causing catastrophic damage to infrastructure, displacing an estimated 340,000 civilians, and disrupting critical supply chains across 12 provinces. The Government of the Philippines requested international assistance on 28 February 2026.

USINDOPACOM activated Operation Pacific Relief on 28 February 2026 in coordination with the Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP), USAID, and coalition partners from Australia, Japan, and the Republic of Korea. The operation concluded initial response activities on 12 March 2026, transitioning to sustained support operations under Philippine government lead.

## 2. TASK ORGANIZATION

## 4. MISSION ACCOMPLISHMENTS

- **Personnel assisted:** Approximately 185,000 displaced persons received direct aid
- **Supplies delivered:** 2,400 tons of food, water, and medical supplies
- **Medical treatments:** 4,200 patients treated at field hospitals and mobile clinics
- **Helicopter sorties:** 347 rotary-wing sorties to isolated communities
- **Airlift missions:** 62 C-130J missions from Kadena and Clark
- **Engineering:** 14 km of roadway cleared; 3 emergency bridges installed
- **Search and rescue:** 892 persons evacuated from flood-affected areas

## 5. KEY OBSERVATIONS

### 5.1 Sustains (What Went Well)

1. **Rapid activation:** JTF-505 achieved initial operating capability within 36 hours of the Philippine government's request, meeting the USINDOPACOM standard of 48 hours.
2. **Coalition interoperability:** Pre-positioned liaison officers from Exercise Balikatan 2025 enabled seamless coordination with AFP units.
3. **Airlift capacity:** The forward positioning of C-130J aircraft at Kadena reduced initial response time by approximately 18 hours compared to CONUS-based assets.
4. **Civil-military coordination:** The embedded USAID DART improved prioritization of aid delivery based on civilian needs assessments.
5. **Communications:** Deployable SATCOM packages maintained connectivity in areas where commercial infrastructure was destroyed.

### 5.2 Improves (Areas for Development)

1. **Logistics tracking:** The manual tracking system for supply distribution created a 12-24 hour lag in situational awareness. Recommend implementing automated logistics tracking for future HA/DR operations.
2. **Language barriers:** Insufficient Waray-Waray and Cebuano interpreters slowed coordination with local government units in the first 72 hours.
3. **Maritime access:** Port damage at Tacloban was more severe than initial assessments indicated, delaying ship-to-shore operations by 48 hours. Pre-storm port surveys should be incorporated into contingency planning.
4. **Medical supply chain:** Critical blood products and surgical supplies ran low on Day 5 due to higher-than-expected trauma cases. Medical logistics planning factors need revision for typhoon scenarios.
5. **Information sharing:** Coalition partners operated on different classified and unclassified networks, creating friction in real-time information sharing. A common operating picture accessible to all partners is needed.
6. **Rotary-wing maintenance:** The high operational tempo resulted in 3 helicopters reaching maintenance limits by Day 8, reducing sortie capacity by 20%.

## 6. RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Invest in automated logistics tracking** for HA/DR operations using commercial-off-the-shelf solutions compatible with coalition systems.
2. **Expand the interpreter pool** through pre-negotiated contracts with regional language service providers and integration of machine translation tools.
3. **Update contingency plans** to include pre-storm infrastructure assessments for key ports and airfields in typhoon-prone areas.
4. **Revise medical logistics planning factors** based on lessons from this operation; increase surgical supply pre-positioning by 30%.
5. **Develop a coalition common operating picture (COP)** accessible on mission partner networks to improve real-time coordination.
6. **Increase rotary-wing maintenance readiness** by pre-positioning additional spare parts and maintenance teams for sustained HA/DR operations.

## 7. CONCLUSION

Operation Pacific Relief demonstrated USINDOPACOM's ability to rapidly respond to a large-scale natural disaster in the Indo-Pacific. Coalition interoperability was significantly improved compared to previous HA/DR operations, attributable to the sustained engagement through exercises like Balikatan and RIMPAC. However, logistics tracking, medical supply chain management, and coalition information sharing remain areas requiring investment and attention.

The lessons from this operation should be incorporated into the next revision of USINDOPACOM's HA/DR contingency plan (OPLAN 5070) and integrated into Exercise Pacific Sentinel 2026 training objectives.
